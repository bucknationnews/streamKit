  • helpWhat is this for?

    The networking behind video streaming is complicated, and it must be done efficiently to Buckhorn's infrastructure from being overloaded. The Buck Nation News team has set up a streaming system that uses multicast, a very efficient way to stream to many computers. However, for this to work, your computer has to open a different stream based on which subnet you are on. A subnet is like a section of a network, and here at Buckhorn subnets are determined based on your position (student, teacher, administrator, and so on). Your computer automatically handles all of this, so the end user never has to deal with it. This simple website will automatically detect what subnet your computer is on and send you the appropriate shortcut to open the stream you need. All you have to do is download the shortcut (a Windows Batch file) and place it on your desktop. If you have VLC Media Player installed, it will automatically connect to the correct stream when you click the shortcut.

  • inputHow do I install the shortcut?

    When you click download, your browser will download a tiny program (the shortcut) that tells VLC media player how to open the stream. You can run this shortcut from anywhere on your computer, but we recommend putting it on your desktop.

  • buildIt's not working for me...

    If nothing happens when you try to launch the program from your shortcut, you probably don't have VLC Media Player installed.On most computers, you can check this by going to Start > All Programs and searching for VLC. If you can't find it, contact Ms. Sullins or a broadcasting student and we will come install it for you.

    This project is released under an open source license on GitHub. It makes use of jQuery, MaterializeCSS, and custom scripts originally written by Hampton Morgan.
